# Content Guidelines - Leading Powerful Conversations Website

## Content Strategy Overview

### Purpose
Create compelling, valuable content that establishes authority in leadership communication while building trust with potential clients and readers.

### Target Audience
- **Primary**: Mid to senior-level leaders seeking to improve communication skills
- **Secondary**: HR professionals and organizational development practitioners
- **Tertiary**: Individual contributors preparing for leadership roles

### Content Pillars
1. **Communication Excellence**: Practical communication strategies and techniques
2. **Leadership Development**: Leadership growth and skill-building content
3. **Organizational Impact**: Stories of transformation and measurable results
4. **Personal Growth**: Self-awareness and emotional intelligence topics

## Writing Standards

### Voice and Tone

#### Brand Voice Attributes
- **Authoritative**: Demonstrates deep expertise and knowledge
- **Approachable**: Warm, accessible, and human
- **Practical**: Focuses on actionable advice and real-world application
- **Empowering**: Inspires confidence and capability in readers
- **Clear**: Avoids jargon, uses plain language

#### Tone Guidelines by Content Type

**Homepage/Landing Pages**
- Confident and inspiring
- Focus on transformation and results
- Use empowering language that motivates action

**Educational Content (Blog/Resources)**
- Helpful and instructional
- Patient and supportive
- Focus on practical application

**About/Bio Pages**
- Personal but professional
- Authentic and relatable
- Demonstrate expertise through storytelling

**Contact/Service Pages**
- Welcoming and accessible
- Clear about process and expectations
- Reassuring about confidentiality and professionalism

### Writing Style Guidelines

#### Grammar and Mechanics
- Use active voice wherever possible
- Write in second person ("you") to engage readers directly
- Keep sentences under 20 words when possible
- Use parallel structure in lists and series
- Follow AP Stylebook for consistency

#### Formatting for Web
- Use short paragraphs (2-4 sentences maximum)
- Include subheadings every 200-300 words
- Use bullet points and numbered lists for easy scanning
- Bold key concepts and takeaways
- Include white space for visual breathing room

#### Language Choices
- **Use**: Clear, specific terms
- **Avoid**: Business jargon, buzzwords, vague language
- **Replace**: "Leverage" → "Use", "Synergy" → "Collaboration"
- **Preferred**: "Leading" vs "Managing", "Conversation" vs "Meeting"

## Content Types and Standards

### Homepage Content

#### Hero Section
- **Headline**: Clear value proposition (8-12 words)
- **Subheading**: Supporting benefit statement (15-25 words)
- **Call-to-action**: Single, clear action (2-4 words)
- **Supporting text**: Brief credibility statement

#### Sections Required
1. **Value Proposition**: What transformation you provide
2. **Credibility**: Brief bio highlights and social proof
3. **Services**: Clear service offerings
4. **Call-to-Action**: Multiple touchpoints for engagement

### Biography/About Page

#### Structure
1. **Opening**: Professional credential and current focus
2. **Background**: Relevant experience and expertise
3. **Approach**: Philosophy and methodology
4. **Personal**: Humanizing details and interests
5. **Call-to-Action**: Invitation to connect

#### Content Guidelines
- Lead with credentials and current work
- Include specific, measurable achievements
- Share personal motivation and values
- Keep professional but show personality
- Update regularly with recent accomplishments

### Resources/Blog Content

#### Article Structure
1. **Headline**: Clear benefit or solution (6-10 words)
2. **Introduction**: Hook and preview (50-75 words)
3. **Body**: 3-5 main points with examples
4. **Conclusion**: Summary and call-to-action
5. **Author bio**: Brief credibility statement

#### Content Requirements
- Minimum 500 words for SEO value
- Include practical, actionable advice
- Use real examples (anonymized if necessary)
- Provide takeaways or key points
- Include internal links to other relevant content

### Contact Page

#### Essential Elements
1. **Clear process**: How engagement works
2. **Response time**: When they can expect to hear back
3. **Contact options**: Multiple ways to reach out
4. **Confidentiality**: Privacy and discretion assurance
5. **Next steps**: What happens after initial contact

## SEO and Discoverability

### Keyword Strategy

#### Primary Keywords
- "Leadership communication"
- "Executive communication coaching"
- "Powerful conversations"
- "Leadership development"
- "Communication skills training"

#### Secondary Keywords
- "Difficult conversations"
- "Executive presence"
- "Leadership coaching"
- "Communication workshops"
- "Team communication"

#### Long-tail Keywords
- "How to lead difficult conversations"
- "Executive communication skills development"
- "Leadership communication training programs"
- "Improve leadership communication style"

### Content Optimization

#### Title Tags (50-60 characters)
- Include primary keyword
- Front-load important terms
- Make compelling and clickable
- Avoid keyword stuffing

#### Meta Descriptions (150-160 characters)
- Summarize page value proposition
- Include call-to-action
- Use active voice
- Include target keyword naturally

#### Header Tags (H1, H2, H3)
- One H1 per page with primary keyword
- Use H2 for main section breaks
- Include related keywords in subheadings
- Maintain logical hierarchy

#### Internal Linking
- Link to relevant pages and resources
- Use descriptive anchor text
- Create content clusters around topics
- Update existing content with new links

## Content Creation Process

### Planning Phase
1. **Audience research**: Understand reader needs and questions
2. **Keyword research**: Identify target terms and topics
3. **Competitive analysis**: Review similar content and gaps
4. **Content outline**: Structure main points and flow
5. **Success metrics**: Define goals and measurement

### Creation Phase
1. **Draft creation**: Focus on value and clarity
2. **Expert review**: Validate accuracy and approach
3. **SEO optimization**: Implement keyword strategy
4. **Formatting**: Apply web-friendly formatting
5. **Internal review**: Check against brand guidelines

### Publishing Phase
1. **Final proofread**: Grammar, spelling, and flow
2. **Technical check**: Links, images, and functionality
3. **Mobile testing**: Ensure mobile-friendly display
4. **Publishing**: Upload and format in CMS
5. **Promotion**: Share across relevant channels

### Review and Update Process
- **Quarterly review**: Update statistics and examples
- **Annual refresh**: Major content updates and improvements
- **Performance tracking**: Monitor engagement and conversions
- **Feedback integration**: Incorporate client and reader feedback

## Content Calendar Guidelines

### Publishing Frequency
- **Blog/Articles**: 2-4 times per month
- **Resource updates**: Monthly
- **Bio updates**: Quarterly or as needed
- **Service page updates**: As services evolve

### Seasonal Considerations
- **Q1**: Goal setting and planning topics
- **Q2**: Team building and collaboration
- **Q3**: Leadership development and skills
- **Q4**: Reflection and year-end planning

### Content Themes by Month
- **Leadership challenges**: Common issues and solutions
- **Communication techniques**: Specific skills and methods
- **Case studies**: Success stories and transformations
- **Industry insights**: Trends and developments
- **Personal development**: Self-awareness and growth

## Quality Assurance

### Content Checklist Before Publishing
- [ ] Aligns with brand voice and tone
- [ ] Provides clear value to target audience
- [ ] Includes relevant keywords naturally
- [ ] Has clear call-to-action
- [ ] Is formatted for web readability
- [ ] Contains no grammatical or spelling errors
- [ ] Includes proper internal and external links
- [ ] Has optimized title and meta description
- [ ] Displays properly on mobile devices
- [ ] Follows accessibility guidelines

### Performance Metrics
- **Engagement**: Time on page, scroll depth, shares
- **Conversion**: Contact form submissions, email signups
- **SEO**: Organic traffic, keyword rankings, backlinks
- **User feedback**: Comments, direct feedback, testimonials

### Review and Approval Process
1. **Self-review**: Creator checks against guidelines
2. **Peer review**: Another team member reviews
3. **Expert approval**: Subject matter expert validates
4. **Final approval**: Brand owner gives final approval
5. **Publishing**: Content goes live with promotion

## Legal and Ethical Guidelines

### Client Confidentiality
- Never use real client names without permission
- Anonymize all case studies and examples
- Obtain written consent for testimonials
- Protect sensitive business information

### Copyright and Attribution
- Only use images with proper licensing
- Attribute quotes and research properly
- Respect intellectual property rights
- Create original content or properly license

### Accuracy Standards
- Fact-check all statistics and claims
- Cite credible sources for research
- Update information as new data becomes available
- Clearly distinguish opinion from fact
